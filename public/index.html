I am Woojin Bae, an undergraduate student at Seoul National University majoring in Chemical Biological Engineering and currently participating in an exchange program at North Carolina State University.
I am particularly fascinated by the synthesis and characterization of eco-friendly materials, with a keen interest in leveraging computational technologies—such as self-driving laboratories and TEM analysis—for their synthesis and structural investigation. My research primarily focuses on next-generation lithium metal batteries, where I analyze the solid electrolyte interphase using Cryo-TEM to improve battery stability and sustainability. In addition, I have explored the synthesis and optoelectronic properties of quantum dots, which has deepened my understanding of advanced materials and their role in green energy solutions.
Beyond academics, I actively foster innovation and collaboration through various forums. My leadership in organizing events and securing partnerships showcases my commitment to impactful change and building connections across diverse communities.
