Tokyo Forum Youth Session 2024

Project Overview

The project, titled "Rethinking Life Choices Beyond the 'Child Penalty'", critically examines the societal and cultural factors contributing to declining birth rates in East Asia, focusing on the exclusionary capitalist notions of productivity, narrow definitions of success, and polarized societal narratives. It highlights how workplace marginalization, urban competition, and governmental propaganda create perceived opportunity costs and mistrust around child-rearing. To address these issues, the project proposes the LifeBridge Program, a participatory curriculum designed to bridge lived experiences with social policies through storytelling, evidence-based lectures, and inclusive roundtable discussions. By fostering empathy, critical reflection, and open dialogue, the program aims to challenge entrenched norms, promote respect for diverse lifestyles, and advocate for systemic changes that value all life choices and labor, ultimately encouraging a more inclusive and equitable society.
