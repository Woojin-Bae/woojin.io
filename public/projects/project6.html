Self-Driving Fluidic Lab

Project Overview

This research focuses on the development of solar concentrator nanoparticles aimed at enhancing the efficiency of solar cells. These nanoparticles are designed to convert ultraviolet (UV) light into visible light, thereby expanding the usable energy spectrum for solar cells. The core material used in this study is manganese-doped perovskite. My research involves optimizing the Mn doping levels and halide (Cl, Br) ratios to achieve the highest possible photoluminescence quantum yield (PLQY), which serves as a key performance metric for these nanoparticles. To streamline the synthesis process, our experimental setup integrates artificial intelligence and robotic arms, enabling precise and automated material fabrication. The research leverages a Genetic Algorithm (GA) to determine the optimal precursor concentrations and synthesis sequences, aiming to maximize nanoparticle efficiency and yield. This work contributes to advancing next-generation solar energy technologies by improving light conversion efficiency and enhancing the overall performance of photovoltaic systems.

Technologies Used

Nanotechnology, Genetic algorithm, Self-Driving Labs
